# Summary of Brian Tracy’s *Eat That Frog!*

*Eat That Frog! — 21 Great Ways to Stop Procrastinating and Get More Done in Less Time* is a practical guide to doing your most important work first. This document is a short summary of its core ideas. This app is built around the same principles.

---

## Why “Eat That Frog”?

If you have to eat a frog, do it first thing in the morning. If you have to eat two frogs, eat the biggest one first.

The **“frog”** is your **most important task** — the one that will bring you the most success. These tasks often deliver five or ten times the value of others. They’re also usually the hardest and most complex. So giving them your best time and focus is crucial.

**You can’t eat every tadpole and frog in the pond.** But you can make sure you eat the **biggest and ugliest frog** first. Doing that sets you up for consistent success.

---

## Three Questions to Ask Yourself

To maximize productivity and “eat your frog” first, ask:

1. **What are my highest-value activities?**
2. **What can I, and only I, do that, if done well, will make a genuine difference?**
3. **What is the most valuable use of my time right now?**

---

## Planning: Think on Paper

- **Decide what you want** — on your own or with your manager. **Write it down.** That’s “thinking on paper.”
- **Set a deadline** for your goal and use **sub-deadlines.** Without deadlines, goals lack urgency.
- **Create a list** of all sub-tasks needed to reach your goals, and keep adding to it.
- **Organise** those sub-tasks into a plan, in the order you’ll do them.
- **Visualise** your plan.
- **Take action** on your plan right away.
- **Build in daily activities** that move you toward your ultimate goals.

*Proper Prior Planning Prevents Poor Performance.*

---

## A Step-by-Step Approach to Planning

1. **Write a list** — Include everything you have to do before you work through the plan.
2. **Work from the list** — Treat it as your reference. When something new comes up, add it to the list first, even if it’s urgent. Add before you do.
3. **Plan ahead** — Create your plan in advance (e.g. the night before). Let your subconscious work on it overnight; better ideas often show up the next morning.
4. **Update the list** — As you work, move unfinished items to the next day’s list and tick off what you’ve completed.

---

## Focus on the Top 20 Percent

You should **refuse to work on the bottom 80 percent** while you still have tasks in the **top 20 percent** left. Practising this consistently builds the habit of tackling the most critical tasks first.

---

## Key Result Areas

**Key result areas** are areas of work for which **you** are entirely responsible. If you don’t do this work, it won’t get done — and the output is often crucial for others’ input. So these areas matter for you and your team.

You can find them by listing your most important **output** responsibilities and asking: *What do others need from me to start their work?* You can also ask others what outputs from you are vital for their work.

---

## Why We Procrastinate — and How to Get Better

A major cause of procrastination is **feeling weak or deficient** in one part of a task. That single feeling can be enough to stop you from starting.

The more you practise, the better you get at “eating” that kind of frog. And the better you get, the more likely you are to start and finish.

**Everything is learnable.**

---

## You Are Unique

You have talents and abilities that nobody else has. The goal of this method — and this app — is to help you use your time and focus where they matter most: on your frog first.

---

## The ABCDE Method (Priority System)

Tracy recommends labelling tasks by impact:

- **A** — Must do; serious consequences if you don’t.
- **B** — Should do; mild consequences.
- **C** — Nice to do; no consequences.
- **D** — Delegate; someone else can do it.
- **E** — Eliminate; drop it if it no longer matters.

Work on A tasks before B, and B before C. This app’s board uses the same A–E priorities so you can focus on frogs first.
